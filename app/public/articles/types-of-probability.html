Joint Probability

This is the probability of two or more events happen together. They are dependent. Example: the probability of finding nucleotide A followed by C. This is the joint probability.

Mathematically, the probability of two events AAA and BBB is denoted as follows...

Independent events

P(A∩B)=P(A)×P(B)P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B)P(A∩B)=P(A)×P(B)

An example might be someone who decides if they are going to take an umbrella to work without checking the weather. Their decision and whether it rains are independent.

Dependent events

P(A∩B)=P(A)×P(B∣A)P(A \cap B) = P(A) \times P(B | A)P(A∩B)=P(A)×P(B∣A)

Where P(B∣A)P(B|A)P(B∣A) is the probability of BBB given AAA already occurred.

An example is the probability of drawing an ace then a king from a desk of cards. If an ace has been drawn, the probability of a king changes.

The probability of drawing a king:

P(Ace)=452P(Ace) = \frac{4}{52}P(Ace)=524​

Now a king, considering an Ace has been drawn:

P(King∩Ace)=P(King)∗Prob(King∣Ace)=452∗451=0.6%\begin{aligned} P(King \cap Ace) & = P(King) * Prob(King| Ace) \\ & = \frac{4}{52} * \frac{4}{51} \\ & = 0.6\% \end{aligned}P(King∩Ace)​=P(King)∗Prob(King∣Ace)=524​∗514​=0.6%​

Not very likely!
